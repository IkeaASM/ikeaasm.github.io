Introduction


What is IKEA ASM dataset?
The IKEA ASM dataset is a multi-modal and multi-view video dataset of assembly tasks to enable rich analysis and understanding of human activities. It contains 371 samples of furniture assemblies and their ground-truth annotations. Each sample includes 3 RGB views, one depth stream, atomic actions, human poses, object segments, object tracking, and extrinsic camera calibration. Additionally, we provide code for data processing, including depth to point cloud conversion, surface normal estimation, visualization, and evaluation in a designated github repository. More information can be found in the paper .


Characteristics

  • 5 assembly environments
  • 4 furniture types
  • 3 furniture colors (white, black and oak)
  • 48 unique assemblers
  • 1:3 female:male ratio
  • 3 RGB views for each assembly
  • 1 depth stream for each assembly
  • 33 action classes (verb-object pairs)

Human pose

We provide human skeleton annotations of the subjects involved in the assembly. The skeleton annotation includes 12 body joints and five key points related to the face. Due to occlusion with furniture, self-occlusions and uncommon human poses, we include a confidence value between 1 and 3 along with the annotation. Due to the size of the dataset, we manually annotate only 1% of the video frames which are selected as keyframes that cover diverse human poses throughout the entire video and provide pseudo ground-truth for the remainder.

Object segmentation
&
tracking

We provide instance-level segmentation annotation of the objects (furniture parts) involved in the assembly. An enclosing polygon is drawn around each furniture part. Due to the size of the dataset, we manually annotate only 1% of the video frames which are selected as keyframes that cover diverse object poses throughout the entire video and provide pseudo ground-truth for the remainder. For the same set of manually annotated frames, we also assign each furniture part with a unique ID, which preserves the identity of that part throughout the entire video.

Atomic actions

We proivde temporal boundary annotations (start and end frame) of all atomic actions in the video from a pre-defined set of actions. Actions are composed of verb-noun pairs indicating the human interacting with specific durniture parts.
